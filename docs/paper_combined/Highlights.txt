Highlights

- A more accurate building surrogate can be a worse RL training environment.
- Temporal coarse-graining, not black-box structure alone, makes a surrogate useful.
- A hybrid (black-box rollouts + frozen-twin reward censor) recovers control.
- The optimal physics-censor weight is controller-family specific.
- Calibration transfers across testcases; frozen-policy transfer is regime-bound.
